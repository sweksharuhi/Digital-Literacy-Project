Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)
General Online Safety
* ? Use strong, unique passwords for each account (mix of letters, numbers, and symbols). 
* ? Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps. 
* ? Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments. 
* ? Check website URLs carefully before entering login or personal details. 
* ? Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software. 
* ? Limit the amount of personal information you share on social media. 
Financial / UPI Safety
* ? Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking details with anyone—even if they claim to be from the bank. 
* ? Remember: you do NOT need to enter a UPI PIN to receive money—only to send money. 
* ? Verify payment requests carefully before approving any “collect request” in apps like Google Pay or PhonePe. 
* ? Avoid scanning unknown QR codes or clicking payment links from untrusted sources. 
Device & Network Safety
* ? Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or sensitive transactions. 
* ? Log out from shared or public computers after use. 
If Something Goes Wrong
* ? Immediately report cyber fraud at the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in 
* ? Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for urgent financial fraud assistance. 
Staying alert and following these simple steps can significantly reduce the risk of cybercrime.






Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

- Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts and update them regularly.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.
- Avoid clicking on unknown links or downloading attachments from untrusted sources.
- Always verify website URLs before entering login or personal information.
- Limit sharing personal details like phone number, address, or college info on social media.
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for banking or sensitive transactions.
- Keep your phone and laptop updated with security patches and antivirus software.
- Log out from shared devices and avoid saving passwords on public systems.

Financial / UPI Safety:
- Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone under any circumstances.
- Remember: entering a UPI PIN is only required to SEND money, not to receive it.
- Double-check payment requests before approving collect requests.
- Avoid scanning QR codes or clicking payment links from unknown sources.

Reporting Cybercrime:
- Report incidents at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- Call Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud)
